Home

The hobby that I chose to build a site about is a bit unconvetional, but what I chose is collecting mil-surp. Mil-surp stands for miliray surplus, and can be catagorized in a few ways. The 2 big catagories are firearms, and equipment. The latter is about items such as clothing, worn gear like vests and backpacks, rations, and packed gear such as bunking material and safety equipment. The Prior is about firearms that at one point were used in military service, but were phased out with better technology. Many are sold or given to smaller or developing contries until they could gather or build their own arms supply for their troops. After that, they are sold to be inported into the US commercial market to be sold commercially. Mil-surp is done both as a way to preserve historical items, as well as for governments to get more worth the phasing out of equipment than gained through the scrapping, trashing, or distruction of equipment. I am a part of the first group, having a collection of historical military firearms.

Where I Look

In the local Charlotte area, there aren't many gun stores, and the stores there are typically either are pawn and gun stores, or are smaller stores with an indoor range. Because of this, there aren't really any stores that I have found with mil-surp in stock, so I go to online sources. I use a mixture of auction sites, as well as online stores to see what is available, and the worth and condition of items. I also use online recources to do research on any firearms that I plan to buy before purchasing. After purchase, firearms are shipped based laws and firearm classification, but for virtually all purchases* (except those exempted as antique through the US gun control act) are shipped to an FFL, or Federal Firearms Lisense, where you undergo a background check by the lisensed entity, and if you pass it, the firearm is transfered into your name. I typically will buy from online stores where I am getting "new" surplus, or surplus that is being sold on the commercial market for the first time.
