The Van's Interior

Current Situation

Currently, the van is bare, with the cargo wall between the cab and the cargo area removed. The cab is in good shape, and it will probably stay as is. Because it is a cargo van, it has no windows, and no temperature control or lighting in the back where the cargo area used to be. All of the interior in the cargo area has been emptied out, and is bare right now.

On the left is a picture of the cab, and on the right is the removed cab wall

The Plan

The cab will be kept the same for the most part, with the only modification being the addition of swivel systems to allow the cab seats to turn 180 degrees from the cab to the living area. For the basics, I will have insulated walls, ceiling, and floor, but haven't figured out the material I am using yet. This is my first time doing something like a van conversion, and plan to use it mostly for recreation like camping, so I am planning to keep it pretty simple.

  • For plumbing, I plan to use a jug system for my fresh and greywater tanks, so they can be removed for filling and emptying. I want to have a full, single piece shower, but for the sink and toilet, I will use something easier, most likely a portable sink style, and a DIY 5 gallon bucket toilet.
  • For electrics, I want to have a solar system with an Eco flow power system, with the ability to add lithium ion batteries to it for a larger power bank. For lighting, I want to have LDE lighting put in at various points of the van.
  • For bedding, I plan to have a cot style bed, with hooks to use for hanging hammoks for additional bedding options.
  • For other plans, I am planning to add one or two airmax fans, along with double plane, hinge opening windows to open up and give the ability to see out of the van, as well as allow for plenty of air circulation through the van, while also keeping the van as insulated as possible. I will also plan to use a gym style interlocking floor.
